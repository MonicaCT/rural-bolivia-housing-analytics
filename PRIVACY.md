# Privacy, ethics and responsible data use

## Public-data rule

Only synthetic, non-identifiable records may be committed to this repository. Original SPSS files,
exports, names, contact details, precise locations and free-text responses belong in approved,
access-controlled research storage and must never enter Git history.

## Why deletion is not enough

Removing a file in a later commit does not erase earlier Git objects, forks, caches or clones. When
personal data have been committed publicly, the response should include visibility restriction,
history rewriting, access review, incident documentation and a new anonymised release.

## Disclosure controls

- Replace direct identifiers with random study IDs before analysis.
- Generalise age, geography and rare occupations where disclosure risk remains.
- Suppress or combine cells with very small counts.
- Review intersections of variables, not just individual columns.
- Publish only the minimum data required to reproduce a result.
- Record consent, lawful basis, retention period and authorised users.

## Analytical ethics

- Report missingness and denominator changes.
- Avoid deficit-oriented labels for communities or age groups.
- Do not infer causality from cross-sectional associations.
- Quantify uncertainty and avoid unstable subgroup rankings.
- Document who may be harmed by an incorrect interpretation.

## Synthetic-data warning

Synthetic data protect the respondents represented by the original study, but they do not validate
the original findings. Every public figure and metric must retain a visible synthetic-data label.
